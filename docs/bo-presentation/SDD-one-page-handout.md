# Spec-Driven Development (SDD)
### Shipping the right thing — predictably.

A simple, repeatable way to turn a business need into working software — with a
human signing off at every critical step. Especially important now that AI can
write code fast: SDD makes sure that speed points in the *right* direction.

---

## The problem it solves

| Today | With SDD |
|---|---|
| Requirements live in chats and people's heads | One approved spec, written in plain English |
| "Is this what we wanted?" found out *after* building | Agreed and approved *before* building |
| Scope quietly grows; timelines slip | Scope changes are explicit decisions |
| AI writes code nobody specced | AI builds against an approved spec |
| Rework discovered late and expensive | Problems caught at the gate, not after launch |

---

## How it flows

**Constitution → Specify → Clarify → Plan → Tasks → Analyze → Implement**

- **Constitution** — the ground rules every feature must respect.
- **Specify** — *what* we're building and *why*, in business language. **← you live here**
- **Clarify** — remove any doubt or ambiguity before planning.
- **Plan** — *how* engineering will build it.
- **Tasks** — the work broken into a tracked checklist.
- **Analyze** — a sanity check that everything lines up.
- **Implement** — build, test, and ship against the approved spec.

Each step leaves a written record you can read and review.

---

## Three approvals keep humans in charge

Work cannot pass a gate until the right person says **"go."** No silent surprises.

1. **Spec approved** — the business owner confirms *what* and *why*. *(before any planning)*
2. **Plan approved** — the tech lead confirms *how*, safely. *(before any tasking)*
3. **Go to build** — an explicit "go" before a single line of code is written.

---

## Who owns what

- **Business Owner / PM** owns the **WHAT & WHY**: sets the ground rules, writes
  and approves the spec, answers questions, approves the go.
- **Engineering** owns the **HOW**: turns the spec into a plan, breaks it into
  tasks, builds and ships, and flags anything that conflicts with the rules.

---

## What it buys the business

- **Predictability** — same path every time; easier to forecast.
- **Less rework** — catch "wrong thing" at the spec, not after it's built.
- **Full traceability** — every piece of work traces back to an approved requirement.
- **AI with guardrails** — use AI's speed safely; it builds only what was approved.
